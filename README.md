# Make an Army memo with AI

A short, practical guide to generating **AR 25-50 compliant Army memorandums (MFRs)** with AI — written for busy clinicians and staff who want a time-saver, not a programming course.

### 👉 Read the guide: **https://black-jl.github.io/mfr-with-ai/**

The guide walks you through three methods, easiest first:

1. **Ask Sage → Overleaf** — the fast path. Browser only, nothing to install, ~15 minutes the first time. *(All most people ever need.)*
2. **Claude Cowork** — a desktop agent that writes the file for you.
3. **Claude Code in VS Code** — the way it's demoed in class; most setup, least effort per memo.

You never learn or edit LaTeX — the AI writes it, you copy and paste.

## Two rules that matter most

- **Mind where the words go.** Ask Sage is CUI-approved; Overleaf and commercial chatbots are not. Keep CUI/PHI/PII out of them — for sensitive memos, compile on your own machine.
- **You sign it, you own it.** AI invents names, dates, and reg numbers confidently. The prompt tells it to leave blanks instead. Read every line.
